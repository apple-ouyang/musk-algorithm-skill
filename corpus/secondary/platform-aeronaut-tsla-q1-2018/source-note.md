# Source Note

## Why it matters

This transcript gives the clearest public case study for Musk's "delete before optimize" logic during the Model 3 ramp.

## Key quotes

- "So we try to automate the placement and bonding of fluff to the top of the battery pack, which is ridiculous."
- "Machines are not good at picking up pieces of fluff. Hands are way better at doing that."
- "One of the questions asked was, do we actually need that?"
- "So we tested a car with and without and found that there was no change in the noise volume in the cabin."
- "So we actually had a part that was unnecessary for which the line kept breaking down."
- "We're going to scrub the barnacles on that front. It's pretty crazy. We've got barnacles on barnacles."

## Use in the skill

Use this source for the strongest public example of:

- questioning a requirement
- deleting a needless part
- avoiding automation of a bad process
